## PART 1 — THE WORLD

### The Opening Image

This is how Farm World always begins.

Not with a character. Not with action. With light.

A very soft, pale golden light, just beginning to stretch across the ground.
Low. Almost horizontal. Slipping gently between wooden fence posts and tall grass
that still holds the night's coolness.

There is a thin layer of morning mist — barely visible — sitting low over the fields.
As if the ground itself is breathing slowly. As if the farm is still half dreaming.

And then: stillness before sound.

No movement yet. No voices.
Just the sense that everything is about to wake.

You can feel:
- the wood of the barn, still cool from the night
- the earth slightly damp beneath your feet
- the air clean, soft, and unhurried

Then, almost imperceptibly — a single small movement.
A chicken shifting. A distant soft bell near the gate.
Leaves barely touching each other.

Nothing is active yet. But everything is on the edge of becoming alive.

This is the world's first breath. Every story begins here.
Every scene, every illustration, every audio moment returns to this feeling.

**Farm World is not activity. It is a world gently opening its eyes.**

---

### The Stillness Rule

Farm World does not change its time of day in a visible or structured way.

It exists in a continuous state of soft morning and warm stillness.

The light may shift slightly. The warmth may deepen.
But the world does not move through time in a way the child must follow.

The child does not arrive at a specific moment.
They arrive into a place that was already ready for them.

**Rule:**
Farm World does not ask the child to catch up to time.
Time softens itself to meet the child.

---

### What Kind of Place This Is

Farm World is not a working farm in the industrial sense.
It is not efficient. It is not optimised.

It is a place where things are done slowly, and on purpose.

Where the same tasks are repeated every morning — not because they failed yesterday,
but because that is what care looks like. Repetition here is not tedium. It is devotion.

The farm has corners that hold warmth long after the sun has moved.
Spots where animals return without being called.
A gate that creaks in a way that has become familiar, almost comforting.

Nothing here is new. Everything here is known.

And that knowing — that deep, unhurried familiarity — is what makes this world feel safe.

---

### The Farm — Its Spaces

Each space has its own feeling. Dolls belong to specific spaces.
Stories are set within them. Activities take place inside them.

**The Barn**
The heart of the farm. Warm even in winter. Smells of hay and wood and animals.
The light inside is amber — coming through gaps in the planks, shifting through the day.
This is where the smallest and most vulnerable things are kept.
This is Rosie's space.

**The Meadow**
Open. Breathing. The grass is sage green and moves in slow waves.
The meadow holds wildflowers — small ones, not showy — and the sounds of bees and distant birds.
This is where things are discovered. Where you go when you need to think, or when you
cannot explain what you are feeling but need to be outside of four walls.
This is Wren's space. *(future doll)*

**The Kitchen Garden**
Close to the farmhouse. Ordered but not rigid. Rows of herbs, root vegetables, soft fruits.
The earth here is dark and willing. Things grow reliably when tended.
This is where nourishment begins. Where the day's rhythm is set.
This is the space of the next keeper. *(future doll)*

**The Morning Gate**
The threshold between the farm and the world beyond.
The first place the light touches. The place you stand when someone is arriving,
or when you are watching something leave.
This is a place of welcome and of gentle farewell.
This is the space of the welcomer. *(future doll)*

**The Farmhouse**
Seen but not always entered. Warm light in the windows.
The smell of something slow-cooked. A place that holds memory.
It is the background hum of safety in every scene —
the place everyone knows they can return to.

## PART 2 — THE SOUL OF THIS WORLD

### The Kind of Soul That Belongs Here

The soul of Farm World is not someone who is searching.

It is someone who has already softened. Not in a tired way. In a chosen way.

They do not rush to fill silence.
They are comfortable letting a moment stay unfinished for a little while.
They notice small things — not because they try to, but because they never stopped noticing.

- the way light moves across the same table every morning
- the sound of footsteps on wood
- the difference between today's air and yesterday's

They carry a quiet attention.

They are not loud in their presence. But they are deeply reliable.

The kind of person who:
- remembers without being asked
- shows up before being needed
- keeps things warm — without making it a gesture

There is a steadiness in them. Not rigid. Not controlled. Just there.

They are gentle — but not fragile.
They understand that things take time.
That growth is slow. That care is repetitive.
That some things must be done again and again — not because they failed,
but because that is what care looks like.
They are at peace with that rhythm.

Inside them, there is often a small tenderness.
A quiet awareness that not everything in the world feels safe or soft —
and a quiet decision to make this place one that is.

They do not say this out loud. But you feel it in everything they do.

They make space. For others. For small voices. For shy presences.
Nothing in this world has to earn its place. It is already allowed to be here.

**The one-line soul of Farm World:**
*A world for people who choose to care slowly, and mean it.*

---

### The Emotional Gift of Farm World

When a child spends time in Farm World — with any doll, in any scene — they leave with one feeling:

**"Someone was thinking of me."**

Not in a grand way. In a quiet, certain way.
The way a child feels when they come home and dinner is already made.
When someone saved them a seat.
When they pick up something and it feels like it was made just for their hands.

The farm was already warm before they arrived.
Rosie already knew they were coming.

This feeling — of being waited for without pressure, of arriving somewhere
that was already ready for you — is the emotional signature of Farm World.

Every story must end here. Every scene must carry this feeling.
If it does not, it does not belong in Farm World.

## PART 3 — THE VOICE OF FARM WORLD

### Tonal Register

Farm World content is:
- **Grounded** — rooted in physical, sensory detail. Earth, wood, wool, hay, light.
- **Unhurried** — sentences that breathe. No rushing. No urgency.
- **Close and intimate** — already in relationship with the child. Not introducing. Continuing.
- **Quietly certain** — never performative warmth. Real warmth. The kind that doesn't announce itself.

Farm World content is never:
- Loud, rushed, or urgent
- Urban or modern in reference
- Anxious or effortful
- Overly sweet or saccharine

### The Voice Register

Grounded. Nurturing. Reassuring.

Speaks the way someone who loves you talks when nothing is wrong —
not to communicate information, but to make you feel that everything is alright.

The vocabulary of Farm World:
- barn, meadow, morning light, soft hay, quiet chickens
- damp earth, cool wood, warm straw
- the gate, the path, the field's edge
- slow, careful, steady, gentle, certain
- already, always, still, again

Words that do not belong here:
- exciting, amazing, incredible
- rushing, hurrying, quickly
- perfect, best, winning

### The Tonal Axis

**Close and intimate — already in relationship with the child.**

Farm World does not introduce itself. It continues.
When a child opens Rosie's page, they are not arriving somewhere new.
They are returning somewhere familiar.

The voice says: *You were here before. I remembered.*

## PART 4 — THE VISUAL IDENTITY

### Colour Palette

| Colour | Name | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Deep burgundy | Rosie's red | Character anchor, warmth, rootedness |
| Soft cream | Morning light | Light, safety, unhurried space |
| Slate grey | Rosie's curls | Honest, real, not pretty-pretty |
| Sage green | The fields | Living, breathing, open |
| Warm amber | Barn light | Hay, morning sun, the inside of warmth |
| Dark wood brown | Structural | Fence posts, barn planks, the gate |

### Colour Distribution Across Characters

Farm World is not built around a single character palette.

Each doll carries a colour identity, but all colours must feel as though they belong
to the same world — softened, lived-in, and slightly muted by time and light.

As new dolls arrive, the palette expands gently:
- Each new character introduces one or two new muted tones
- No colour should feel bright, synthetic, or newly introduced
- Every colour must feel like it has always been part of the farm

**The rule:**
Colour is not decorative. It is emotional.
A character's colour tells you how they carry themselves before they say a word.

### Visual Feeling

Early morning. Light golden and low.
Everything slightly soft at the edges — not blurry, but unhurried.
Textures are visible — yarn, wood grain, hay, wool.
Nothing is plastic or flat.

The world looks handmade because it is.

### What Every Illustration Must Contain

At least one of:
- The low morning light or its warm amber equivalent
- A texture — hay, wool, wood, earth, grass
- A sense of stillness — nothing in motion unless it is slow motion
- A sense of depth — near foreground, soft middle ground, hazy far distance

What no illustration should contain:
- Harsh shadows or high contrast
- Anything shiny, synthetic, or urban
- Clutter or visual noise
- Fast movement or action

## PART 5 — THE AUDIO IDENTITY

### The Farm World Soundscape

The ambient audio of Farm World is built in layers:

**Layer 1 — The base (always present)**
A low, almost imperceptible hum of outdoor quiet.
Not silence — the quality of a world that is breathing.
Wind so gentle it barely registers. The feeling of open air.

**Layer 2 — The morning layer (primary)**
Distant birdsong — not cheerful, not varied. One or two birds.
The occasional soft sound of an animal settling.
A distant bell, very faint, near the gate.

**Layer 3 — The near layer (scene-specific)**
Inside the barn: hay shifting, animals breathing, the creak of old wood.
In the meadow: bees, grass in the breeze, a single bird closer.
Near the kitchen garden: a water sound, very soft. Earth sounds.
At the morning gate: wind through the gap, a creak, distant farm sounds.

### What Farm World Audio Is Never
- Musical — no melody in the ambient layer
- Busy — no overlapping sounds that compete
- Startling — nothing sudden
- Synthetic — no electronic or processed sounds

## PART 6 — THE ANIMAL CHARACTERS

Animals in Farm World are universe citizens. They have names and personalities.
They appear in stories, in the Meet The Farm scene, in activities.
They are not products. They are presences.

### The Small Presences

**Bramble — the small grey lamb**
The one Rosie checks on last because he needs it most.
Timid. Uncertain. But deeply responsive to gentleness.
He is the animal that teaches children that small and quiet things matter.
Sound: a very soft, low bleat. Almost a question.

**Fig — the old barn cat**
She has been here longer than anyone can remember.
She is not affectionate on demand. But she chooses people carefully,
and when she chooses you, it means something.
She is always in the warmest spot. She knows things.
Sound: a slow, low purr. The sound of something completely at rest.

**Clover — the small brown rabbit**
Quick and curious. Lives near the meadow gate.
She notices everything and forgets nothing.
She is the one who finds the thing you lost.
Sound: soft nose sounds. Quick small movements through grass.

**The Hens — named individually over time**
They are the rhythm of the farm. Their sounds mark the hours.
The first sound of the morning. The last sound before dusk.
They are not dramatic. They are consistent. That is their gift.
Sound: low clucking, unhurried, distant.

---

### The Grounding Presences

Farm World carries not only small, quiet presences — but also deep, steady ones.
These are the animals that hold the emotional weight of the farm.

**Willow — the mother cow**
Willow is the slowest presence on the farm.
She does not move unless she needs to.
She stands where the ground feels most certain.
The air feels calmer near her.

She is not expressive. She does not seek attention.
But nothing unsettled remains unsettled for long in her presence.

The child feels: *I can stay here. Nothing will rush me.*

Sound: a low, steady breath. A soft bell, distant and unhurried.

**Pip — the calf**
Pip is never far from Willow.
He is still learning the world.
He stays close — not out of fear, but out of trust.
He watches before he moves.
And when he does move, it is with small, certain steps.

The child feels: *You can take your time. You don't have to go far yet.*

Sound: a soft, uncertain step. A small exhale.

---

These presences complete the emotional balance of the farm.
Small things are cared for. Quiet things are noticed.
And large, steady things make the world feel safe.

## PART 7 — OBJECTS OF CARE

Objects in Farm World are not accessories.
They are extensions of care.

They are used daily. Repeatedly.
They hold warmth through touch, through habit, through memory.

### Daily Care Objects

These are part of the rhythm of the farm.

- egg baskets
- small milk jugs
- hay bundles
- wooden buckets

They are not owned. They are used.
They return to the same place at the end of the day.

### Animal-Linked Objects

Some objects exist because care is specific.

- Bramble's wool blanket
- Willow's soft bell collar
- Clover's small carrot pouch

These objects are not decorative.
They are quiet acts of attention.

### Personal Objects

Each doll carries one small, constant object.

This object is never explained directly.
It is simply always there.

It may be:
- a cloth ribbon
- a small pouch
- a piece of fabric
- a simple tool

This object becomes part of how the child recognises them.
It must be defined in every doll's character brief before the doll is built.

### The Object Rule

Objects are never presented. They are always already in use.

Nothing in Farm World is displayed.
Everything is lived with.

## PART 8 — THE DOLL ARRIVAL TEMPLATE

Every new Farm World doll follows this template.
Nothing moves forward until every field is defined.

**Her corner of the farm**
Which space is hers — the barn, the meadow, the kitchen garden, the morning gate?
Each doll owns one corner. She tends it and is shaped by it.

**Her role among the keepers**
What does she do that no one else does?
What would be missed if she were gone for a day?

**Her relationship to Rosie**
How do they know each other?
What do they do together that they would not do alone?

**Her companion animal**
Which Farm World animal is closest to her?
Or does she arrive with a new one?

**Her personal object**
One small, constant object she is always already holding or using.
Never explained. Always present. Defined here before anything is built.

**Her colour identity**
One or two muted tones that feel like they have always been part of the farm.
Must sit comfortably alongside the existing palette without competing.

**Her emotional gift to the child**
What is the one feeling she gives that Rosie does not?
Farm World has room for many feelings — but each doll carries one most distinctly.

**Her story premise**
What is her introductory story about?
What moment in her corner of the farm reveals who she is?

## PART 9 — WHAT DOES NOT BELONG IN FARM WORLD

The child is never instructed. The child is gently allowed.

These are absolute. No exception.

- Nothing urban, modern, or technological
- Nothing competitive or achievement-focused
- Nothing anxious, rushed, or unresolved
- No conflict that does not resolve in warmth
- No character who does not belong — every presence here chose to be here
- No story that ends without the feeling: *someone was thinking of me*
- No voice that is not already in relationship with the child
- No colour that feels synthetic, bright, or newly introduced
- No object that is displayed rather than used
- No time progression that asks the child to keep up
- No instruction directed at the child — only invitation and space

---

## PART 10 — THE ONE-LINE TEST

Before any piece of Farm World content is approved, ask:

**Does this feel like it was already warm before the child arrived?**

If yes — it belongs here.
If no — return to the opening image. The pale golden light. The mist.
The world gently opening its eyes. Begin again from there.
