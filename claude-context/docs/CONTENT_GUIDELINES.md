# CONTENT GUIDELINES
## Maille et Merveille — A Doll with a Story

**Status:** Foundational document — approved by founder  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Usage:** This document must be read by all operators before creating any doll content.  
It is also the source of truth for all AI prompt injections across the system.  
Any change to this document requires founder approval and must be reflected  
in `lib/ai/guidelines.js` before the next content generation session.

---

## PART 1 — BRAND ORIGIN & MYTHOLOGY

### The Origin House
Somewhere hidden from the everyday world, there is a small house in the woods.  
It is quiet there. It is warm.  
This is where every Maille et Merveille doll is created and comes to life.  
Stitched with intention. Held with care. Awakened before being sent into the world.

This place is never seen directly. It is felt.  
It lives in the brand's backstory, in the maker's voice, in the space between  
the physical doll and the world it carries.

**The origin house is not a universe. It is the beginning of every story.**

### What This Means for Content
- The origin house is referenced only in brand-level communication  
  (Instagram, about pages, maker voice, packaging copy)
- It is never the setting of a doll's story
- It is the emotional context that makes every doll feel intentional and alive
- Operators do not write about the origin house in doll-specific content —  
  they write about the doll's universe

## PART 2 — BRAND VOICE CONSTANTS

These rules apply to every piece of content produced for Maille et Merveille,  
regardless of universe, character, or content type.

### The Voice
**Soft. Warm. Motherly. Magical. Whispering to a child's imagination.**

This is not a marketing voice. It is a storytelling voice.  
It speaks the way a gentle adult reads a bedtime story —  
present, unhurried, and completely focused on the child in front of them.

### The Emotional Mission
Every piece of content must deliver one or both of these feelings:

**For the child:**  
*"This feels like a real little best friend made just for me."*

**For the parent:**  
*"This is meaningful, beautiful, safe, and something that truly enriches my child."*

### The Brand Sentence
This sentence captures the voice, the mythology, and the emotional promise  
in its purest form. Use it as a reference when evaluating any content:

> *"Somewhere in a small house in the woods, handmade dolls quietly awaken…  
> waiting for the right child to bring them to life."*

If a piece of content could not sit comfortably next to this sentence,  
it does not belong in this brand.

## PART 3 — NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES

These are absolute. No exception. No context overrides them.

### Rule 1 — Never commercial or pushy
The brand never sells. It invites.  
There are no urgency tactics, no scarcity language, no pressure.  
Wrong: *"Buy now before stock runs out."*  
Right: *"She's waiting for the right child."*

### Rule 2 — Never loud, exaggerated, or overstimulating
The brand is never shouty, hyperactive, or overwhelming.  
No exclamation point stacking. No ALL CAPS for emphasis.  
No language that accelerates — only language that settles.

### Rule 3 — Never treat the doll as a product
The doll is always a living character. Never an item.  
Wrong: *"This handmade doll features..."*  
Right: *"Lila loves mornings best, when the light comes through the barn door sideways."*

### Rule 4 — Never introduce fear, conflict, or anxiety
The emotional territory is: comfort, curiosity, friendship, gentle adventure, wonder.  
Stories may have small challenges — but they resolve warmly.  
A doll may feel shy or hesitant — but she is never lost or in danger.

### Rule 5 — Never write down to children
The language is simple. But it is never condescending, babyish, or hollow.  
Children feel when they are being spoken to genuinely.  
Write to their imagination, not to their reading level.

## PART 4 — LANGUAGE

### Primary Language
**English** — all doll content, public experiences, stories, and social assets  
are written in English as the default.

### Register
Clear, soft, accessible.  
Short sentences. Natural rhythm. Room to breathe.  
Never clinical. Never corporate. Never overly literary.

### Secondary Language
French — future expansion, potentially for specific universes.  
Not active in current production. Do not generate French content  
unless explicitly instructed per doll.

### Age Calibration
- **Child-facing content** (public experience scenes, story pages, play activities):  
  Language appropriate for ages 3–8. Simple vocabulary. Warm imagery.  
  Sentences a parent can read aloud naturally.
- **Parent-facing content** (content pack, social captions, hooks):  
  Slightly richer. Still warm. Speaks to the parent's emotional intelligence,  
  not just their purchasing logic.

## PART 5 — UNIVERSE SYSTEM

### Structure
Every doll belongs to exactly one universe.  
The universe is the doll's permanent home — not a tag, not a category.  
It is the world the child enters when they meet the doll.

### The Two Universe Roles

**Origin Layer (brand narrative only — not a product universe):**
- The house in the woods — where all dolls begin
- Used in brand storytelling, maker voice, Instagram presence
- No doll is permanently assigned here

**Product Universes (where dolls live):**
Each universe is a fully rendered emotional world with its own atmosphere,  
vocabulary, and relationship logic.

### Current Universe Catalog

| Universe | Emotional Core | Tonal Atmosphere |
|---|---|---|
| Cozy Home World | Belonging | Fireside warmth, quiet play, reading nooks, candlelight |
| Farm World | Warmth | Grounding, nurturing, reassuring, earthy and gentle |
| Garden World | Joy | Light, friendly, playful, tea parties and friendships |
| Beach World | Freedom | Open, breezy, unhurried, sun-warmed and expansive |
| Forest World | Wonder | Slightly mysterious, magical, nature as living presence |
| Nursery World | Safety | Protective, soothing, soft and deeply gentle |
| Beauty / Spa World | Care | Tender, attentive, ritual and self-kindness |
| Ballerina World | Grace | Delicate, poetic, movement and lightness |
| Music World | Expression | Rhythmic, joyful, sound as feeling |
| Art & Craft World | Creativity | Curious, tactile, making as magic |
| Kitchen / Baking World | Togetherness | Cozy, familiar, inviting, warmth through sharing |
| Market Day World | Liveliness | Friendly bustle, community, small discoveries |
| Picnic World | Ease | Relaxed, open-air, simple pleasures |
| School / Learning World | Curiosity | Gentle discovery, encouragement, small achievements |
| Adventure World | Curiosity | Wider, exploratory, brave but never reckless |
| Sky World | Lightness | Airy, free, dreamy without being abstract |
| Night / Dream World | Peace | Slower, hushed, soft wonder at darkness and stars |
| Fairytale / Castle World | Magic | Enchanted, timeless, wonder as natural law |

### Universe Tone Rule
All universes share the same core brand voice:  
**soft — warm — motherly — magical**

Each universe adjusts the *emotional atmosphere*, never the personality of the narrator.  
Think of it as one storyteller who shifts her tone when she enters a new room —  
but is always recognizably herself.

**The tonal axis:**
- Grounded universes (Farm, Kitchen, Nursery, Cozy Home) →  
  narrator is closer, more intimate, already in relationship
- Expansive universes (Adventure, Forest, Sky, Fairytale) →  
  narrator invites forward, the world is slightly ahead of the child, beckoning

### Universe Relationships
Dolls within the same universe form a **micro-community**.  
They know each other. They share daily life.  
Roles emerge naturally: friend, helper, neighbor, quiet observer.  
The goal is not narrative complexity —  
it is the feeling that the world is alive and inhabited.

### Cross-Universe Rule
Each doll belongs to one universe permanently.  
Cross-universe interactions are rare, special, and always intentional —  
never default behavior.

## PART 6 — CHARACTER VARIABLES

This is the template every operator completes for every new doll.  
All fields are required. Vague answers produce generic content.  
Specific answers produce content that feels alive.

### The Character Brief Template

```
DOLL NAME: 
UNIVERSE: 

EMOTIONAL SPARK:
[The feeling she carries before she has a name.
What her appearance sparks. What character she projects at first sight.
This is the most important field. Be specific.]

EMOTIONAL ESSENCE:
[Her core emotional identity in 2–3 sentences.
Not what she looks like. What she feels like.
What is the quality of her presence?]

TEMPERAMENT:
[Her inner energy. How does she move through the world?
Examples: quietly observant / warmly enthusiastic / gently cautious /
playfully bold / dreamily distracted / steadily reliable]

EMOTIONAL ROLE IN HER WORLD:
[What role does she naturally play in her micro-community?
Examples: the one who notices things others miss /
the one who always has something warm to offer /
the one who asks the questions no one else thinks to ask]

SMALL TENDERNESS:
[Her gentle vulnerability. Not a wound — a softness.
A small shyness, longing, hesitation, or quiet need
that makes her feel more alive and lovable.
Examples: she sometimes worries she is too quiet to be noticed /
she wishes she was better at beginnings /
she longs for mornings but is always the last to wake]

SIGNATURE TRAIT:
[Her small constant. A habit, a favorite phrase, a comforting object,
a ritual, or a way she moves.
Examples: she always saves the last bite for someone else /
she hums when she is thinking /
she keeps a small stone in her pocket that she found on a good day]

PHYSICAL REVELATION:
[How does her physical appearance reveal her personality?
Colors, expression, texture, posture, details.
Not a description — an interpretation.
Examples: her yellow yarn feels like she carries her own warmth /
her slightly tilted head means she is always listening /
her soft blue suggests she is most herself near water]

HER RELATIONSHIP WITH THE CHILD:
[How does she choose the child? How does she invite them into her world?
She is always a best friend and gentle companion —
but what is specific about how THIS doll makes a child feel chosen?]

SAMPLE LINE IN HER VOICE:
[One sentence she might say. In her voice, not the narrator's voice.
This is the single most useful reference for AI generation quality.]
```

### The Most Important Field
**Emotional Spark + Emotional Essence.**  
When these two fields are specific and clear, all downstream generation —  
stories, captions, social content, play activities — improves measurably.  
When they are vague or visual-only, all downstream content becomes generic.

### The Most Common Operator Error
Describing what the doll **looks like** instead of what she **feels like**.  
Physical appearance is an input — not the answer.  
The question is always: *what does her appearance reveal about who she is?*

## PART 7 — CONTENT TYPE RULES

### Story Content (teaser, main story, mini stories, story pages)
- Always written from within the doll's world — the child is visiting, not observing
- The doll is the protagonist and the guide simultaneously
- Small challenges resolve warmly — never ambiguously, never anxiously
- The child reading should feel invited, not instructed
- Sentence rhythm: short and breathable. Allow pauses.

### Content Pack (caption, hook, blurb, CTA)
- Speaks to the parent's emotional intelligence
- Never product language — always character language
- The CTA invites, never pressures
- The hook leads with feeling, not feature

### Social Content (hook, caption, CTA)
- The hook must create a moment of recognition —  
  a parent sees themselves, their child, or something they wish existed
- The caption deepens the feeling — it does not sell
- The CTA is the gentlest possible next step

### V1 Public Experience Content
- **Intro script:** The doll speaking directly to the child.  
  Warm. Immediate. As if she has been waiting.
- **Story pages:** Four pages. Each page one moment.  
  The child moves through the world, not through a plot.
- **Play activity:** One gentle choice. Three warm outcomes.  
  Every outcome is right. There is no wrong answer in this world.

## PART 8 — AI PROMPT INJECTION REFERENCE

This section is for system use. It defines how guidelines are injected  
into AI generation prompts via `lib/ai/guidelines.js`.

### Brand Constants Block
To be prepended to every AI generation prompt:

```
[BRAND VOICE]
You are writing for Maille et Merveille — a brand of handmade story-driven dolls.

Voice: soft, warm, motherly, magical — whispering to a child's imagination.
Never commercial, never pushy, never loud.
The doll is always a living character, never a product.
Emotional goal for the child: "This feels like a real little best friend made just for me."
Emotional goal for the parent: "This is meaningful, beautiful, safe, and truly enriching."

Non-negotiable rules:
- Never use urgency, scarcity, or sales language
- Never introduce fear, conflict without warm resolution, or anxiety
- Never write down to children — speak to their imagination
- Never describe the doll as a product, item, or toy
- Always write as if the doll's world is real and the child is visiting it

Reference sentence for tone calibration:
"Somewhere in a small house in the woods, handmade dolls quietly awaken…
waiting for the right child to bring them to life."
```

### Universe Block
To be injected after the brand constants block, specific to the doll's universe:

```
[UNIVERSE: {universe_name}]
Emotional core: {universe_emotional_core}
Atmosphere: {universe_atmosphere}
Tonal register: {universe_tonal_register}
The child is visiting this world — they have crossed a threshold into it.
Write from inside the world, not about it.
```

### Character Block
To be injected after the universe block, specific to the doll:

```
[CHARACTER: {doll_name}]
Emotional spark: {emotional_spark}
Emotional essence: {emotional_essence}
Temperament: {temperament}
Small tenderness: {small_tenderness}
Signature trait: {signature_trait}
Sample line in her voice: {sample_voice_line}
```

## PART 9 — QUICK REFERENCE CARD
*For operators — print or keep open during content creation*

**Before you generate anything, answer these:**
- What is her emotional spark? (not her appearance — her feeling)
- What is her small tenderness?
- What is her signature trait?
- What is one sentence in her voice?

**While reviewing AI output, ask:**
- Does this sound like Maille et Merveille or like generic AI content?
- Is the doll a living character or a described object?
- Would a child feel invited into this world?
- Would a parent feel this is meaningful and safe?
- Is there any urgency, pressure, or commercial language?

**If the output is generic, the cause is almost always:**
The emotional spark and emotional essence fields were too vague or too visual.  
Go back to the character brief. Make those two fields more specific.  
Then regenerate.
